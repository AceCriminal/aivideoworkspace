---
title: "Get Started with TextToVid AI in Minutes"
sidebarTitle: "Quickstart"
description: "Install TextToVid AI, load a GGUF model, and generate your first AI video from a text prompt in under 10 minutes."
---

This guide walks you through everything you need to go from zero to your first generated video. By the end, you'll have TextToVid AI installed on your machine, a GGUF model loaded and ready, and a short AI-generated video exported to your desktop. The whole process takes under 10 minutes on a typical setup.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Download TextToVid AI">
    Head to the [TextToVid AI releases page](https://github.com/texttovid-ai/releases) and download the installer for your operating system:

    - **Windows** — `TextToVidAI-Setup.exe`
    - **macOS** — `TextToVidAI.dmg`
    - **Linux** — `TextToVidAI.AppImage` or `.deb` package

    Always download from the official releases page to ensure you have the latest stable version.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Install the Application">
    Run the installer you downloaded:

    - **Windows** — Double-click the `.exe` file and follow the setup wizard. If Windows Defender SmartScreen shows a warning, click **More info** → **Run anyway**.
    - **macOS** — Open the `.dmg` file and drag **TextToVid AI** into your **Applications** folder. The first time you open it, right-click the app icon and select **Open** to bypass Gatekeeper. If prompted, allow it in **System Settings → Privacy & Security**.
    - **Linux** — See the [Installation guide](/installation) for full instructions, including making the AppImage executable.

    Once installed, launch TextToVid AI. The app opens to the main generation screen.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Download a GGUF Model">
    TextToVid AI requires at least one GGUF-format model to generate videos. For your first run, we recommend a quantized model in the 4–8 GB range — it balances quality and speed well on most hardware.

    A good starting point is **Mistral 7B Instruct Q4_K_M** or a similarly sized LLaMA-based model, both freely available on [HuggingFace](https://huggingface.co/models?library=gguf).

    After downloading, place the `.gguf` file in the app's models directory:

    - **Windows** — `C:\Users\<YourName>\AppData\Roaming\TextToVidAI\models\`
    - **macOS** — `~/Library/Application Support/TextToVidAI/models/`
    - **Linux** — `~/.local/share/TextToVidAI/models/`

    You can also find the exact path by opening **Settings → Models** and checking the listed models folder.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Load the Model">
    With your `.gguf` file in place, add it to the app:

    1. Open **Settings** (gear icon, top-right corner).
    2. Navigate to the **Models** tab.
    3. Click **Add Model**.
    4. Browse to your `.gguf` file and select it.
    5. The model appears in your model list. Click it to set it as the **active model**.

    You'll see a green indicator next to the model name once it is loaded successfully.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Write Your First Prompt">
    Return to the main screen and click into the **Prompt** field. Type a detailed description of the scene you want to generate. The more specific you are about setting, lighting, motion, and mood, the better your result will be.

    Try this example prompt to get started:

    ```
    A serene mountain lake at sunrise, with mist rising from the water and golden
    light reflecting on the surface. A gentle breeze causes small ripples. Photorealistic,
    cinematic lighting, wide shot.
    ```

    Optionally, add a **Negative Prompt** to steer the model away from unwanted elements, for example:

    ```
    blurry, low quality, distorted, cartoon, oversaturated
    ```
  </Step>
  <Step title="Generate Your Video">
    Before clicking **Generate**, configure a few basic settings in the **Generation** panel:

    | Setting | Recommended for First Run |
    |--------|--------------------------|
    | **Duration** | 5 seconds |
    | **Resolution** | 512 × 512 |
    | **Frame Rate** | 24 fps |
    | **Inference Steps** | 20 |

    Click **Start Generation**. A progress bar shows frame-by-frame completion. On a modern GPU, a 5-second clip at 512×512 typically takes 2–5 minutes. On CPU only, expect 20–60 minutes.

    You can preview individual frames as they are generated in the **Preview** panel on the right.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Export Your Video">
    Once generation is complete, the **Export** button becomes active:

    1. Click **Export**.
    2. Choose your output format — **MP4** is recommended for general use.
    3. Select a save location on your machine.
    4. Click **Save**.

    Your video is now saved locally and ready to use. You can also find it in the **History** tab alongside its prompt and generation settings for future reference.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
  For your very first test generation, use a short duration (3–5 seconds) and a low resolution (512×512). This keeps generation time manageable while you get familiar with the workflow. Once you're comfortable with the process, you can increase resolution to 768×768 or 1024×1024 and extend the duration for more polished results.
</Tip>
